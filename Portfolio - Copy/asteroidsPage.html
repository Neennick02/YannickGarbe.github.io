Asteroids Storm

About

This game is inspired by the 1979 arcade classic Asteroids with a modern spin.
Shoot asteroids to break them up and collect powerups for more satisfying hits.

Project Info

Engine: Unity
Target Platform: PC / WEBGL
Development time: 3 weeks
Team size: 1 developer

Introduction.

Asteroids is a singleplayer space shooter with basic fun mechanics and 5 different powerup types.
Split asteroids by shooting them, after all pieces have been destroyed a new round starts and more asteroids appear.
Boost around and destroy asteroids to generate coins and powerups.
Defeat endless waves with increasing chaos and try to beat your personal best.

Gameplay

Control the player and boost to increase speed.
Asteroids can be broken up in smaller peaces and have to be destroyed to move on to the next wave.
All powerups include:

Health up,
Double Shot,
Slow Down Time,
Firerate up,
Homing missiles
All top scores are saved and displayed to the player.

Systems I worked on.

  • Player Movement System – Player is only able to move forward, rotate and boost.

  • Powerup System – Pickup and use powerups

  • ScreenWrap – When the player or asteroids leave the screen they wrap around the screen.

  • Wave difficulty scaling – I made use of the Fibonacci sequence to increase the amount of asteroids spawned per wave.

  • Controller support – Support for both keyboard and controller schemes.

  • Score Board – Save players scores and display when a new high score is set.

  • Particle Systems – Enable Particles on different health stages player.

What I've learned.

While working on this game I had a lot of fun working with basic particles.
By enabling smoke and fire particles when the player can notice that he is almost game over.

Before this project I've never made a highscore system and I noticed that this gives players a reason to play again.

I learned a lot about controller support for Unity, by using the old Input System I made things a little bit harder for myself but in the end I was satisfied by input on controllers.

This was the first project where I used inheritance and I enjoyed making the different powerup types.

When I made the slow down powerup I decided to update the global delta timescale value,
the downside is that this causes the gameplay to feel slow and unresponsive.
