Overkill Protocol

About

In this game you explore a infected space ship and look for a item to open the door to the final room.
Buy upgrades with PCB boards to buy upgrades in the shop.

Project Info

Engine: Unity
Development time: 4 weeks
Team size: 2 developers, 3 artists

Introduction.

In this top-down twin-stick shooter game, you play as a corrupted robot, fighting against the robots that are defending their spaceship against mutants and corrupted entities like yourself. Destroy enemies, collect their systems, and use them to get stronger and destroy even more.

Gameplay

Control the player with the WASD keys and aim with the mouse.
Dash in the movement direction with the shift button and defeat enemies by shooting with the mouse button.
Collection PCB boards dropped by enemies to buy temporairy powerups at the shop panel.
All powerups include:

  • Multishot
  • Time slowdown
  • Extra life
  • Firerate up

    • Systems I worked on.

    • Player Movement System –Twinstick style movement with dash mechanic

    • Enemy Behaviour – Navmesh agents that lock onto player position when in range and view.

    • Combat System – Player attacks and hit detection.

    • Shop System – Removes resources from player inventory and adds powerup.

    • Powerup System – All different powerup types

    • Health Systems– Updates player and enemy health

    • Minimap System – Live updates on player position and show points of interest.

    What I've learned.


    In this project I worked together with another developer for the first time,
    he worked on the interaction system and turret enemies which gave me a lot of the to work on a lot of mechanics.

    A lot of the things implemented are thing I made before like the shop, powerups and health systems but I was able to improve and refactor some aspects.

    During the production the map designer used a lot of time to tweak the main scene which prevented me from implementing and testing things.
    this taught me how important it is to make prefabs from assets, and have clear communication to prevent git conflicts.

    The minimap was a last minute idea I had which in my opinion improved the game a lot because without it it's easy to get lost in the rooms.
