Splunge

About

Splunge is a 2D game inspired by itch.io, dodge enemy projectiles and pickup slime to grow your character.
Taking damage shrinks the player, if the slime bar in the bottom is empty the player is game over.
After growing a couple times the level changes and new enemy types are introduced.

Project Info

Engine: Unity
Target Platform: PC / Poki.com / WEBGL
Development time: 4 weeks
Team size: 2 developers, 3 artists

Introduction.

Splunge is a 2D game inspired by itch.io, dodge enemy projectiles and pickup slime to grow your character.
Taking damage shrinks the player, if the slime bar in the bottom is empty the player is game over.
After growing a couple times the level changes and new enemy types are introduced.

Gameplay

Move around with the arrow keys and dash with the space bar.
Dashing gives a temporairy shield in the direction youre moving.
Moving outside the bounds loops the player to the other side.

Dodge enemy bullets and wait for them to disappear or move into them to push them out of the screen.

Collect slimes to fill the slime bar and make the map grow, after a set amount of grows new enemies appear and the background changes.
Taking damage depletes the slime bar, if the bar is empty the player dies.
When the map grows the slime bar gets partially cleared.

Systems I worked on.

  • Player Movement System – Basic movement and dash

  • Shield Mechanic – Only protects in the direction the player is moving

  • Shield Powerup – Drops random and completely protects the player

  • Screen Wrap – Moving outside the bounds wraps the player to the other side.

  • Enemy Types – All enemy attack patterns except for the fishes

  • Gameloop – Detect when the slime bar is depleted

  • Enemy Spawn System – Grid based system so enemies won't spawn in the same place.

  • Grow/ Shrink Mechanic – Updates out the camera, screen wrap, enemy spawn positions, movement speed and slime bar.

What I've learned.

This project was part of a exchange project with South Korean studens, the team consisted of 1 Korean artist, 1 Korean developer, 1 dutch artist and me.

Because this game was made for Poki.com we were able to playtest regulairly to test features and see if players understood the gameloop.

In the beginning the growing mechanic partially started as a bug but we decided to turn this into the main mechanic.
This taught me that trusting the process is very important and changing a mechanic during production is possible if the current design is not working.

While making the enemies we had the idea to make them all different colors to make the behaviour more predictable,
in the end we decided that making them different shapes would also help making the game more readable.

Making this game and working together with people from abroad was a really fun experience and helped me communicate properly in another language.
