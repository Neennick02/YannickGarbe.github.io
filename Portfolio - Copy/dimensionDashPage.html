Dimension Dash

About

Dimension Dash is a fast Subway Surfers inspired local multiplayer game in which you travel through unique dimensions and overcome obstacles using dash moves and portals.

Project Info

Engine: Unity
Development time: 4 weeks
Team size: 3 developers, 3 artists

Introduction

Dimension Dash is a fast-paced 3D multiplayer mayhem game inspired by the endless-runner style of Subway Surfers, but with a competitive twist. Players race head-to-head through shifting dimensions, dodging obstacles, sliding under barriers and weaving between hazards at high speed.

Gameplay

Player automatically run and has to jump, slide or move into another lane to dodge obstacles.
The movement speed automatically increase over time.

After a set amount of time the players run into portal which teleport the players to the next dimension.
In new dimensions the difficulty increases more until one of the players fails to keep up.

Collect item to punish the opponent or boost your character.
All item types include:

  • Bomb - lands in front of the opponent

  • Spikes - lands in front of the opposite player

  • Flip Controls - Flips the controls for the opponent

  • Flip Position - Flips the z positions of the players

  • Shield - Protects for getting hit by obstacles

  • Speed up - Boosts the player forward

    • Systems I worked on.

    • Player Movement System – physics-based jump & dash.

    • Player inventory system – pickup and use items.

    • Powerup items – all different powerup types.

    • Item drop chances – setup different powerup spawn rates depending on player position.

    • 2 player support – support for 2 players with different control schemes.

    • Gameloop – detection which player is winning and speeding up road over time.

    • Road generation – trigger new road when the road reaches player.

    • Player animation controller – control smooth animation based on player input

    What I've learned.

    During this project I learned a lot about team work, I dicided to make a cooperation agreement contract so the entire team had some ground rules and to prevent miscommunications.

    This was my first experience with making a local multiplayer game, checking for different types of control schemes was hard in the beginning.

    Designing and programming the items was exciting and not to challenging but deciding the spawn rates based on the current position was harder to balance.

    We added the coin as a collectable but there we were not sure how to implement them, this taught me that making a resouce also requires a good use for it.
