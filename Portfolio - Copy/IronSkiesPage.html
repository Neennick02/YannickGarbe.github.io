Iron Skies

About

Dimension Dash is een snelle 2D platformer waarin je door unieke dimensies reist en obstakels overwint met dash-moves en portals.

Project Info

Engine: Unity
Target Platform: PC / WEBGL
Development time: 4 weeks
Team size: 1 developer, 6 artists

Introduction.

Iron Skies is a single player 3D sidescrolling shoot em up.
Control the players height and rotate to accurately aim at enemies.
Collect scraps that drop from defeated enemies to upgrade your vessel in between waves.
Increasing the score drops powerups with boost like Damage up, Health up, Scrap up and FireRate up.

Gameplay

The player controls a ship that has to fight its way through multiple rounds of enemies.
Shoot bullets with space and drop bombs with L shift.

The enemy types include :
helicopters, hot air balloons, zeppelins, small and bigger boats.
Enemies drop scrap that can be used in the shop at the start of every wave.
Upgrade health, speed or add a second barrel to make the waves easier.

The wave bar in the top-right corner shows when the final boss will appear. After the final round a boss appears with more health.

Systems I worked on.

  • Player Movement System –Simple up and down movement.

  • Shop System – Upgrade Health, barrels and movement speed.

  • Powerup items – all different powerup types.

  • Enemy movement – Dynamic movement class used for all enemy types.

  • Enemy attack patterns – Different attack types per enemy.

  • Parallax effect – Moves background elements at different speeds.

What I've learned.

During the production I had a lot of fun making the movement mechanics for the player and making the different enemy types.

This was the first group project I worked on and it learned a lot about working in a Scrum team.
Pitching, sprints, standups and sprint reviews were all new to me and helped me to better reflect on my work.

This project was also the first time we did multiple playtest, which teached me a lot about dealing with feedback and making the game easy to read for new players.

In the end I was really proud with the work I was able to do in such a short project.
